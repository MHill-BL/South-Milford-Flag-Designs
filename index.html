South Milford Flag Proposals

South Milford is a growing village with a rich history but no unifying symbol. A village flag can provide a shared identity, a sense of pride, and a way for the community to rally together. These proposals aim to capture the character of South Milford in a simple, memorable design that residents can recognise and celebrate.

Proposals to finally give the great village of South Milford, Yorkshire a flag. Explore the drafts below and share feedback.

Design 1: Orange Sunset Over the Mill Wheel

The lower third is a deep charcoal (#2b2b2b), containing a mill wheel that reflects South Milford’s milling history and the richness of its farmland. Above it, the upper two-thirds are a bold orange (#f4581b), representing the glow of sunrise and sunset over the village fields, as well as the energy and warmth of the community.

Design 2: Yorkshire Skies and the Mill

The flag is divided vertically into two bold fields. The left third is deep charcoal (#2b2b2b), containing a mill wheel motif that recalls South Milford’s milling heritage and the richness of its farmland. The right two-thirds are Yorkshire blue (#00adef), drawn from the county flag and symbolising both South Milford’s place in Yorkshire and the clear skies above the village.
